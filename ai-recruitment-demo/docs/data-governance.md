# Data Governance Policy — Hire Screening 3.0

**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** Hire With Ali — Compliance Team
**Last reviewed:** 2026-05-28
**EU AI Act reference:** Article 10 (Data and data governance)

---

## Purpose

This policy describes how Hire Screening 3.0 processes, stores, and governs personal and operational data in compliance with the EU AI Act Article 10 and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All data ingested by the Hire Screening 3.0 API
- All data written to audit and operational logs
- All data sent to the Groq API for inference

---

## Data We Process

| Data type | Where collected | Purpose | Lawful basis |
|-----------|----------------|---------|--------------|
| **CV text** | `POST /upload-cv` — extracted from uploaded PDF or DOCX | Candidate scoring input | Legitimate interest (recruitment) |
| **Job description text** | `POST /score-candidate` — submitted by recruiter | Scoring context | Legitimate interest (recruitment) |
| **AI scores (0–100)** | Generated by LLM inference | Ranking and recruiter decision support | Legitimate interest |
| **Score reasoning** | Generated by LLM inference | Explainability; EU AI Act Article 13 transparency | Legal obligation |
| **Recruiter override decisions** | `POST /override-score` | Human oversight audit trail; EU AI Act Article 14 | Legal obligation |
| **Scoring latency (ms)** | Measured at inference time | Performance monitoring | Legitimate interest |

---

## Data We Do NOT Process

The following data items are explicitly excluded from the scoring pipeline:

- **Candidate names and contact details** — CV text is passed to the LLM without prior stripping; however, recruiters are instructed not to include names in the `cv_text` field submitted to `/score-candidate`. A future release will add an automated PII-stripping step.
- **Protected characteristics** (age, gender, ethnicity, religion, disability status, pregnancy) — the system prompt explicitly instructs the LLM to ignore such information if present.
- **Original CV files** — the raw PDF/DOCX binary is not persisted; only the extracted plain text is logged and scored.
- **Recruiter identity** — recruiter actions are logged via `candidate_id` provided by the caller; the system does not authenticate or log recruiter usernames at this time.

## Retention

| Data | Retention period | Location | Deletion method |
|------|-----------------|----------|----------------|
| Extracted CV text (in audit log) | 5 years | `logs/audit_log.json` | Secure file deletion on schedule |
| AI score + reasoning (in audit log) | 5 years | `logs/audit_log.json` | Secure file deletion on schedule |
| Human override records | 5 years | `logs/audit_log.json` | Secure file deletion on schedule |
| CV parse events | 5 years | `logs/cv_log.json` | Secure file deletion on schedule |
| Raw uploaded CV files | 30 days | Not persisted by this system (handled by caller) | N/A |
| MLflow run metrics | 2 years | MLflow tracking server | MLflow experiment deletion |

Retention periods are set to meet the EU AI Act Article 12 requirement that logs are kept for a period appropriate to the intended purpose, with a minimum of six months after the system is placed on the market.

---

## Data Transfers to Third Parties

| Recipient | Data transferred | Purpose | Legal basis |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|-------------|
| **Groq, Inc. (USA)** | CV text + job description (per scoring request) | LLM inference | Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — Groq DPA |

No candidate data is transferred to any other third party. MLflow logging runs locally or within the organisation's own infrastructure.

---

## EU AI Act Reference

This policy implements the data governance requirements of **EU AI Act Article 10**, specifically:

- Article 10(2)(a): Data collection, design choices, and labelling procedures are documented above
- Article 10(2)(b): Data pre-processing operations (text extraction, PII handling intent) are described above
- Article 10(3): Training, validation, and test datasets — not applicable (no fine-tuning; pre-trained model used via API)
- Article 10(5): Special categories of personal data — the system is designed to avoid processing protected characteristics; see "Data We Do NOT Process" above
